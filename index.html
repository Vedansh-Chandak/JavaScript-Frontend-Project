Indian Yuga & Meditation

In ancient Indian philosophy, the Yugas represent four ages‚ÄîSatya, Treta, Dvapara, and Kali‚Äîeach with varying levels of spirituality. During the Satya Yuga, meditation was natural, but as time progressed through the Yugas, meditation became essential to maintain inner peace and spiritual growth. In Kali Yuga, where virtues decline, practices like dhyana (meditation), pranayama (breath control), and mantra chanting became crucial for self-realization and overcoming distractions. The Bhagavad Gita emphasizes meditation as a path to transcend the material world and achieve spiritual liberation.
